# Ringworld culture building

Notes for the delta-dwelling culture whose language seeds the Ringworld
setting. Aelaki itself is a descendant / later stratum; this page covers
the **original culture** — its language names, sound system, grammar,
numerals, writing history, and sample phrases.

## Language names

* **Kxaki** — "delta language". The everyday speech.
* **Kxakxa** — "delta-of-deltas". The prestige variety, spoken in the
  city of the same name (the setting's equivalent of Rome).
* **Maki** — "mother language" / "river language". The older form from
  which Kxaki descends.

## Phonology

### Vowels

Eight emic vowels, arranged on a front/back × close/mid/open grid:

* close: **i**, **u**
* near-close: **ɪ**, **ʊ**
* close-mid: **e**, **o**
* open: **æ**, **ʌ**~**ɑ**~**ɔ** (romanised *a*)
* also **ÿ** /y/ (close front rounded), found in some roots.

Diphthongs do not exist: two adjacent vowels are always split by a
glottal stop. ʔ is treated as the absence of an onset rather than a
phoneme in its own right.

**w and y are allophones** of a single approximant. The realisation is
chosen to contrast with the two adjacent vowels ("inverse colouring"):
*iwi* vs. *oyo*. At syllable codas the approximant forms diphthong-like
glides.

### Consonants

38 consonants, organised across three places of articulation (bilabial,
alveolar, velar) plus the glottal stop. Each place has:

* **Plosive** voiceless / voiced / implosive: p b ɓ · t d ɗ · k g ɠ
* **Nasal**: m · n · ŋ
* **Affricate** voiceless / voiced: pɸ bβ · t̠ʃ d̠ʒ · kx ɡɣ
* **Fricative** voiceless / voiced: f v · s~ʃ z~ʒ · x~h ɣ
* **Click** voiceless / voiced / nasalised: pʘ bʘ mʘ · tǃ dǃ nǃ · kǁ ɡǁ ŋǁ
* **Approximant**: w · r l · (no velar approximant)

With 8 vowels and an empty onset slot, the syllabary gives roughly
**304 attested syllabics**.

## Grammar

* **Alignment**: tripartite — subject, object, and intransitive are
  each marked distinctly.
* **Morphology**: agglutinative.

### Nouns

* **Singular** is unmarked.
* **Exhaustive plural** — suffix *-l*. Indicates *all* members of some
  arbitrary group ("all the X").
* **Non-exhaustive plural** — reduplication. Indicates *some* members,
  not necessarily all.

### Verbs

* **Intransitive plurality agreement** — verbs agree with subject
  plurality only when intransitive.
* **Telicity** is marked.
* **TAM**: tense (past / present / future), aspect (perfective /
  imperfective / habitual), mood (realis / possibility / necessity).
* **Evidentiality and mirativity** are marked as speaker reactions,
  distinct from tense/aspect/mood.

## Numerals

The system is **base-12**, built around the rings (*wiv*) visible in the
sky.

| # | Word |
|---|------|
| 1 | pam |
| 2 | bel |
| 3 | ɓad̠ʒ |
| 4 | mɪ |
| 5 | pfym |
| 6 | bvæ |
| 7 | fÿnǃ |
| 8 | vup |
| 9 | p'ib |
| 10 | b'el (also "two hands" — the hand-dual form) |
| 11 | m'akx ("extra") |
| 12 | wiv ("rings") |

A separate set of glyphs exists as an **alternative financial numeral
system**: same pronunciation as the everyday tallies but different
characters, deliberately harder to alter one digit into another.

## Writing system

Four historical stages, each growing out of the one before it:

1. **Tomb text** — the earliest form, found in ancient tombs. Almost
   entirely logographic, with heavy use of rebus.
2. **Temple text** — phono-semantic compounds emerge and replace most
   rebus writing; glyph shapes are simplified.
3. **Diplomatic text** — common characters are simplified into a
   kana-like phonetic script.
   * *3.5 Late diplomatic text*: kana are absorbed back into new
     phono-semantic compounds (modelled on the historical Khitan script).
4. **Mercantile text** — kana are replaced by a hangul-like syllable
   block system; older kana survive only inside legacy characters.
   * *4.5 Late mercantile text*: newer characters have the
     hangul-like structure built in from the start.

The number **12** / *wiv* is written as a pictogram of two arches —
interpreted as "the house of houses", with a single-arch "house"
character as its base.

## Sample phrases (stream of consciousness)

* **bakx** — interjection, roughly "what?"
* **Zallax Kxa!u** — "Go to the delta/marsh!" (*Zal-* go, *-lax*
  imperative, *Kxa* delta/marsh, *!u* allative marker).
* **ɓoɓox** — reduplicated imperative of *ɓo* "go"; emphatic, "go do
  that thing, do it!"
* **Killax !ÿ'conlang'!u** — "Speak in your conlang." (*!ÿ* "you" →
  glyph <big>ꔌ</big>.)
* **Zunn!a** — "Me?"
* **Zɪn'la** — "I see." (*zɪn* see, *-la* declarative.)
* **ʘawiv zɪnna zunɓi** — "I see the great rings in the sky." A fixed
  phrase for religious awe at the ringworld itself (*ʘawiv* "great
  twelve"; *-na* conjugates the verb for awe; *-ɓi* agentive).
* **ŋornǃÿ** — the name of the planet.
* **Hippo** — a kind of fish.

## Miscellaneous lexicon

* **ɓÿw'wiv** — "day-ring", i.e. clock; captures the idea of the sun's
  passage around the sky against the visible rings.
