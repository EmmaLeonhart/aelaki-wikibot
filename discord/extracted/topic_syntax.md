I think the part of the syntax that most differs from anything existing in English now is adverbs and converbs

The simple form of an adverb is relatively straightforward . But you can also use the comparative to say that something is more a certain trait than another thing, and you can use the superlative to say that it is the most of a certain trait.

Which I think is simple enough but also it is mixed with the fact that the only thing that differentiates a verbs converbs and adverbs is word order so if you miss an aan between two verbs then you suddenly instantly get a quite different converb phrase
